Indicates a consonant cluster.

There appears to be a significant difference in the usage of jazm from other Arabic orthographies, in that it appears above and is stored after the second character in the consonant cluster. This means that the base character may associated with both a vowel diacritic and the jazm in memory, eg. واریُْل

In the SIL Awami Nastaliq font, the inverted-v shape is the default.
