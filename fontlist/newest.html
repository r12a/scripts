Notes

The default text used is article 1 of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, taken from this Unicode page, wherever I could find it. I created a few instances myself, where it was missing, and occasionally I resorted to arbitrary lists of characters.

In some cases, a square box with a cross in it is displayed (this only occurs in the text view if you have the Adobe NotDef font installed). This indicates that the font doesn't cover all the glyphs needed to represent the sample text. Most often this applies to punctuation or other common characters. In normal use in a Web browser, glyphs for such characters will be backfilled by using a glyph from some other font.

All fonts are set to 28px in size. They are roughly grouped with other similar fonts, eg. modulated, monochrome, naskh, nastaliq, no-circle, slanted, kai, etc. 'Modulated' means that strokes have varying widths (and usually have tapered or pointed ends), whereas 'monochrome' fonts have strokes that are generally the same width (and stroke ends are usually squared off).
