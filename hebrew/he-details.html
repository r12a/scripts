׳

Hebrew equivalent of a period in abbreviations.

Also used to modify consonants to create non-native sounds.

The following are found in slang and loanwords that are part of the everyday Hebrew colloquial vocabulary:

  • ʧ with צ or ץ, eg. ריצ׳רץ׳
  • ʤ with ג , eg. ג׳וּק
  • ʒ with ז , eg. זָ׳רְגוֹן
  • w as non-standard orthography for transcriptions, eg. עָוָנְטָה. This sound can also be written using װ [U+05F0 HEBREW LIGATURE YIDDISH DOUBLE VAV]

Geresh is also used to transcribe other sounds not in the hebrew phonology when transliterating (rather than adoption via loanwords).

  • ð with ד, ie. Arabic ذ, eg. ד׳ו אל-חיג׳ה d´v̇ ʔl-xyg´h Dhu al-Hijjah (ذو الحجة). Also used for the English voiced th. Sometimes just ד is used.
  • θ with ת or ץ, ie. Arabic ﺙ, eg. ת׳רסטון ṫ´ʁstv̇n̽ Thurston
  • χ with ח, ie. Arabic خ, eg. שייח׳ ṡyyx´ Sheikh (شيخ). Used to specify χ rather than the possible h of ח
  • ʁ with ר, ie. Arabic غ, eg. ר׳ג׳ר ʁ´g´ʁ Ghajar
