င

U+1004 MYANMAR LETTER NGA

See in UniView

Burmese consonant

ŋ , eg. ငါး ŋá fish.

Vowel nasalisation in final position in native syllables, eg. ဝင် wɪ̀ɴ enter.

When this is the first consonant in a consonant cluster and non-word-final, it rises over the second letter and keeps its virama, rather than pushing the second consonant below it, eg. အင်္ဂလန်. This is called 'kinzi' ကင်းစီး kɪ́ɴzí.

Before Unicode 5.1 it was occasionally difficult to automatically detect whether a kinzi followed by a medial consonant spans two syllables or not. This was fixed in Unicode 5.1. The required sequence of characters to represent a kinzi is:

င [U+1004 MYANMAR LETTER NGA]
 ် [U+103A MYANMAR SIGN ASAT]
 ္ [U+1039 MYANMAR SIGN VIRAMA]
ဂ [U+1002 MYANMAR LETTER GA] (or whatever else follows the kinzi)

Burmese consonant

Unlike other nasals, this is silent in syllable final position, eg. အမည်း. Compare with ဉ [U+1009 MYANMAR LETTER NYA], which does produce nasalisation at the end of a syllable, ie. ညဉ် ɲɲ̇ˣ ɲɪ̀ɴ, and ညည်း ɲɲˣ² ɲí.

အ

U+1021 MYANMAR LETTER A

See in UniView

Description in the Unicode standard:

• also represents the glottal stop as a consonant

Burmese consonant / initial vowel

ʔ as a vowel support sign. The combination of this character and a vowel sign is the normal native way of showing syllable initial vowels. For instance, အိ is the standard Burmese way to represent an initial i vowel. Examples: အိတ်, အားကစား, အုန်းသီး, အောက်တိုဘာ.

As a syllable initial vowel without any other vowel sign attached it sounds like:

a in open syllables, eg. အရိပ်, although this is often reduced to ə.

ɪ, ɛ, a, ɪɴ, aɴ in closed syllables, per the following table, where the end consonant in the rhyme is shown across the top:

Dependent vowel signs

ါ

U+102B MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN TALL AA

See in UniView

Burmese vowel sign

Shapes like ဝာ wa are hard to distinguish from shapes like တ ta, so this taller alternative to  ာ [U+102C MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN AA] is used where needed to avoid visual confusion. Whether alone or as part of a complex vowel, it is used after the following consonants: ဝ ခ ဂ င ဒ ပ, eg. ပေါင်. Where there is no ambiguity, however, the normal shape is used, eg. ပြောင်းဖူး.

On its own:

a Used only in open syllables. Low tone by default, eg. တံဂါ. High tone with visarga, eg. တံခါး.

For creaky tone use inherent vowel, instead.

With  ေ [U+1031 MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN E] (အေါ)

The vowel signs surround the base character.

ɔ in open syllables. High tone by default, eg. tbd; Creaky tone with low dot, eg. ပေါ့တယ် ပေါ့တယ်.

Use asat for low tone, eg. ဒေါ်လေး. Note how the asat combines in a special way with the shape.

aʊ in closed syllables. Usually combines with only the following finals, က င. Low tone by default, eg. သန်းခေါင်. Creaky tone with low dot, eg. ပေါ့တယ်. High tone with visarga, eg. ခေါင်း. (Stopped tone with the stop final, eg. ပြတင်းပေါက်. )

As an initial vowel this is usually အေါ but some words, particularly Indian loan words or words in the literary style, use the following alternative single characters: ဩ [U+1029 MYANMAR LETTER O] for high tone, eg. ဩဂုတ်; ဪ [U+102A MYANMAR LETTER AU] for low tone.

ာ

U+102C MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN AA

See in UniView

Burmese vowel sign

Shapes like ဝာ wa are hard to distinguish from shapes like တ ta, so a taller alternative to this character,  ါ [U+102B MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN TALL AA], is used where needed to avoid visual confusion. Whether alone or as part of a complex vowel, it is used after the following consonants: ဝ ခ ဂ င ဒ ပ, eg. ပေါင် pàʊɴ thigh. Where there is no ambiguity, however, this normal shape is used, eg. ပြောင်းဖူး.

On its own:

a Used only in open syllables. Low tone by default, eg. ဆရာ. High tone with visarga, eg. နွား.

For creaky tone use inherent vowel, instead.

With  ေ [U+1031 MYANMAR VOWEL SIGN E] (အော)

The vowel signs surround the base character.

ɔ in open syllables. High tone by default, eg. ထောပတ်သီး. Creaky tone with low dot, eg. ပျော့တယ်.

Use asat for low tone, eg. ပျော်တယ်.

aʊ in closed syllables. Usually combines with only the following finals, က င. Low tone by default, eg. ကြောင်. Creaky tone with low dot, eg. စောင့်တယ်. High tone with visarga, eg. ကျောင်း. (Stopped tone with the stop final, eg. နောက်လ. )

As an initial vowel this is usually အော but some words, particularly Indian loan words or words in the literary style, use the following alternative single characters: ဩ [U+1029 MYANMAR LETTER O] for high tone, eg. ဩဂုတ်; ဪ [U+102A MYANMAR LETTER AU] for low tone.

Virama & asat

U+1039 MYANMAR SIGN VIRAMA

See in UniView

Description in the Unicode standard:

= killer (when rendered visibly)

Burmese virama

Kills the inherent vowel and produces a stacked conjunct cluster.

In many multi-syllabic words (mostly derived from Pali), consonants that have no intervening inherent vowel are arranged such that the consonant cluster is stacked. The second consonant appears below the first, eg. မန္တလေး, and ဗုဒ္ဓ bud͓d̊ Buddha. The virama character is used between consonants to signal that they should combine in this way, but it is not visible in the end result.

Note that as of Unicode 5.1 this character is no longer used to indicate syllable final consonants with a visible asat character. For that use  ် [U+103A MYANMAR SIGN ASAT].

်

U+103A MYANMAR SIGN ASAT

See in UniView

Description in the Unicode standard:

= killer (always rendered visibly)

Burmese mark, əθaʔ အသတ်

Used over any of the syllable-final consonants when no stacking takes place, eg. စပ် ṡpˣ saʔ.

This character was introduced in Unicode 5.1 and replaces the former use of U+1039 MYANMAR SIGN VIRAMA plus U+200C ZERO WIDTH NON-JOINER to achieve the same effect. For stacked consonant clusters use U+1039 MYANMAR SIGN VIRAMA.

This character is also used in combination with other characters to produce a vowel plus tone combination, eg. ဘယ်. When used this way it is called ʃḛtʰó ရှေ့ထိုး.

It is also sometimes used in Pali or Sanskrit words with characters other than those above.
