This page lists characters in the following Unicode block and provides information about them.

  • Tai Le

See also the companion document, Tai Le/Tai Nüa, for a summary of the UCAS script and its use for the Inuktitut language.

Phonetic transcriptions should be treated as an approximate guide, only. Many are more phonemic than phonetic, and there may be variations depending on the source of the transcription.

Tone letters

The first tone, pitch 33, has no marker, eg. ᥛᥥ.

ᥰ

U+1970 TAI LE LETTER TONE-2

See in UniView

Tai Nüa

55 pitch.t

Tone letter introduced in 1988, during the 4th script reform, that appears after all previous letters in a syllable.

Occurs in open syllables, eg. ᥛᥣᥰ.

The corresponding diacritic introduced during the 3rd reform, in 1963/1964, is ̈ [U+0308 COMBINING DIAERESIS].w

General sources: Unicode, Wikipedia

ᥱ

U+1971 TAI LE LETTER TONE-3

See in UniView

Tai Nüa

11 pitch.t

Tone letter introduced in 1988, during the 4th script reform, that appears after all previous letters in a syllable.

Occurs in open and checked (ie. ending in -p, -t, -k) syllables, eg. ᥛᥣᥱ, ᥞᥨᥐᥱ.

The corresponding diacritic introduced during the 3rd reform, in 1963/1964, is ̌ [U+030C COMBINING CARON].w

General sources: Unicode, Wikipedia
