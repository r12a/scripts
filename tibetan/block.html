འ

U+0F60 TIBETAN LETTER -A

See in UniView

Tibetan consonant, 'a-chung

à

Used as a prefix before 10 consonants, eg. འཁོར་ལོ་.

a when used as a suffix.

May also nasalise the juncture of two morphemes, as in དགེ་འདུན་, pronounced ɡenyn.

Can be used as a base for dependent vowel signs, in which case the a sound is replaced by that of the vowel. It has a low tone (cf. ཨ [U+0F68 TIBETAN LETTER A]).

Other than loanwords, Tibetan only allows diphthongs in diminutive expressions. 'A-chung is used to write these, as in the following: མི་ > མེའུ་; རྡོ་ > རྡེའུ་.

Can be used to disambiguate the location of an inherent vowel in a syllable. The sequence དག་ is interpreted as CVC. To express CCV add 'a-chung, eg. དགའ་.

Subjoined form is  ྰ [U+0FB0 TIBETAN SUBJOINED LETTER -A].

Refs: Daniels, p431-436, The Unicode Standard 6.3, pp325-344

U+0F6A TIBETAN LETTER FIXED-FORM RA

See in UniView

Tibetan fixed form variant of consonant

Used only in transliteration of non-Tibetan texts, not in normal Tibetan text.

ར [U+0F62 TIBETAN LETTER RA] at the top of a stack usually has a reduced form, eg. རྐ rka (rka). For transliterations it is sometimes desirable to retain the full form of RA where in Tibetan words it would be reduced. To do this use this character instead of the normal RA, but only where the normal RA would not produce the full form anyway, ie. do not use eg. རྙ rnya (rnya), which has the full form already.

Standard form is ར [U+0F62 TIBETAN LETTER RA].

See also  ྼ [U+0FBC TIBETAN SUBJOINED LETTER FIXED-FORM RA].

Refs: The Unicode Standard 6.3, p329

Tibetan lenition mark, tsa-'phru

Should be used immediately after the consonant it modifies, even if that consonant is followed by a subjoined consonant.

This is an integral part of the three consonants ཙ [U+0F59 TIBETAN LETTER TSA], ཚ [U+0F5A TIBETAN LETTER TSHA] , and ཛ [U+0F5B TIBETAN LETTER DZA]. Also used to represent tsa , tsha , and dza in abbreviations.
